 Telecom Customer Churn Prediction & Revenue Impact Analysis
                     
Project Overview
Customer churn is one of the biggest challenges faced by telecom companies, directly impacting revenue and growth.
This project focuses on analyzing customer behavior to identify churn drivers, predict customers likely to leave, and quantify revenue loss due to churn, enabling data-driven retention strategies.

The project combines exploratory data analysis, feature engineering, predictive modeling, and business impact analysis to deliver actionable insights.

 Business Objectives
Identify key factors contributing to customer churn

Predict customers with high churn probability

Analyze revenue loss caused by churn

Provide actionable recommendations to reduce churn and improve retention

 Dataset
Source: IBM Telco Customer Churn Dataset

Access Method: Loaded directly via public dataset URL using Pandas



Key Features:

Customer tenure

Monthly & total charges

Contract type

Payment method

Internet & service subscriptions

Churn status (Target Variable)

Month-to-month contracts show significantly higher churn rates

New customers are more likely to churn than long-tenure customers

Higher monthly charges increase churn probability

Certain service combinations contribute more to churn
